# Individualized Aging Curves Reveal That No Two NBA Players Evolve the Same Way

## Hook

Every NBA player ages, but not on the same timeline, and not at the same rate. Understanding exactly when and how a player will decline is one of the most valuable yet least answered questions in professional basketball.

## Problem Statement

Conventional wisdom in the NBA treats players aging as a universal process, with players developing in their early twenties, peaking around 26 or 27, and gradually declining from there until retirement. Yet, an increasing number of players in the modern era, exemplified by the likes of LeBron James, Kevin Durant, and Steph Curry, have sustained near-peak performance well into their thirties. Despite this, most NBA fans and evaluators still rely on population-level aging trends, applying the same preconceived expected trajectory to every player regardless of their individual history. This leaves teams making hundred-million dollar contract decisions based on beliefs that may be wildly incorrect for the specific player they are signing.

## Solution Description

This project builds an individualized aging curve for every player in the NBA, using their career history, position, and injury record to project how their performance will change year over year. The result is a player-specific forecast that shows whether a player is on track to age gracefully or is showing signs of imminent decline. This gives NBA team decision makers a more complete understanding of players' futures, allowing them to make smarter, better informed decisions about the future of their teams.
